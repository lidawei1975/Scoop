## 1. Abstract

This disclosure describes a volumetric measuring scoop engineered to eliminate product waste and dead zones within cylindrical containers. Unlike traditional round scoops, this design features a flat bottom and straight vertical walls that conform precisely to cylindrical container shapes, ensuring complete product recovery.

---

## B. Method of Operation

When inserted into a cylindrical container, the scoop is manipulated so that its flat bottom floor sits flush against the container's bottom floor, while its straight vertical walls align with the container's interior walls. This design eliminates the common "dead zones" found in round scoops.

---

## Background and Intent

### Why I am sharing this

Twenty years ago, as a parent dealing with expensive infant formula, I was deeply frustrated by a universal design flaw: standard round scoops leave a persistent, wasteful residue at the bottom of containers due to dead zones that cannot be accessed.

### My goal

I want to ensure that this common-sense, cylinder-matching design remains completely free and accessible. My goal is to create permanent "prior art" so that no corporation can ever monopolize this simple yet effective solution.

### A note on prior art

I am an independent citizen and a hobbyist, not a patent attorney. If a similar design or patent already exists in some hidden corner of industrial manufacturing, I sincerely hope this disclosure helps redirect focus to where it belongs—on solving the real problem rather than protecting patents.
